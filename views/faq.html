BikeCoin Project

FAQ Document

1. What is the project about?

BikeCoin is a blockchain platform and protocol which supports bike sharing and urban mobility. The current focus is on sharing of 'smart bikes' which can be hired from service providers such as hotels, resorts and co-working spaces.

2. What is a smart bike?

A smart bike is one which is enabled with GPS and near field communications, so that a rider can trigger the locking system remotely, for example using an app running on a mobile phone. With the addition of a display, smart bikes are able to provide navigational information. Smart bikes are typically powered by a dynamo mounted in the front wheel hub.

3. Why is the service needed?

Bike sharing is a popular service in many cities, but it is centralised, operated either by a private company or government agency. It lacks scalability because the fleet operator must deploy bikes in every city. So called dockless-sharing systems limit the cycling equipment to inexpensive and durable bikes. With BikeCoin, we can enable multiple merchants to collaborate on service delivery and we can offer high-end smart bikes for round trip rides.

4. What is meant by 'round trip' rides?

Bike sharing has gotten a bad name because it is designed for 'last mile' rides, such as one way from the train to work or home. This leads to irresponsible 'ride and dump' behaviours, which result in vandalism and clutter on city streets. Our bike platform supports responsible ridership and round trip rides where the rider is obligated to return the bike to the retailer or another custodian.

5. Why use the blockchain for this application?

Aside from the auditable integrity, and decentralised architecture, blockchain technology enables the network effects that will allow us to create a service which can scale quickly. Bike suppliers, fleet operators and retail service providers collaborate in a bike sharing ecosystem. New services can be launched anywhere, anytime. Riders pay and merchants earn tokens. Merchants can set their own prices. Developers can make use of the BikeCoin protocol to harness the services of the platform, either for bikes or other urban mobility devices such as e-scooters.

6. What is your market positioning?

There are 4 interlinked markets in the supply chain for bike sharing: bicycle supply, fleet operations, custodianship and retail services. We provide an underlying platform that allows merchants to serve a specialised market - such as a local fleet operator, or they can perform multiple roles. The most vertically oriented arrangement would be an individual owner renting to an individual rider (similar to AirBnBs direct model). Our BikeCoin ecosystem can grow without limit, across geographies and to embrace other forms of urban mobility.

7. Who is the intended user?

The BikeCoin platform will provide service to individual riders and groups. Individuals would be able to hire bikes using a decentralised app, either P2P from a bike owner, or from a retail service provider. When renting from providers such as hotels and resorts, the rider does not need to use crypto for payment. The platform supports 'merchant pays' in such situations, which again will help the scalability of the services.

8. Who are your competitors?

Mobike, Ofo and other dockless bike sharing operators are a competitors at the low end of the market, but they cannot support high-end bikes and are not based on blockchain. Ofo is exploring blockchain for tracking only, not commerce. Their expansion is constrained due to regulations designed to limit the negative social effects of one-way rides. Boutique bike rental services such as one finds in resorts is another competitor, but they don't have a scalable business model. Blockchain startups like DOVU are focusing on urban mobility, but do not have a bike sharing model. Pedal offers a gamified blockchain biking app. All of these are nascent projects that don't provide a complete ecosystem.

9. What is your competitive advantage?

BikeCoin has a 2-year head start based on partnership with smart bike supplier Volata Cycles. Volata already makes high-end bikes which are able to integrate into the blockchain. Also, BikeCoin has an experienced blockchain development team.

10. How is blockchain used in the project?

Blockchain is used to support asset management, tracking and commerce. Each bike has an identity on the blockchain, using an asset token, and each bike's location is tracked on the blockchain. This makes it difficult to steal a bike. Each bike has a wallet address. Riders pay into the bike wallet, which accumulates and then redistributes revenue-share to the other merchants. BikeCoin also has various advanced cryptographic features that enable high transaction speed and the protection of confidential information.

11. What is the consensus algorithm used by the project and how was it picked?

The recording of assets is performed on the Ethereum blockchain, using proof of work consensus protocol. This is for transparency and to anchor our ERC20 token. Transactions are handled on our own proprietary chain (based on Ethereum source codes) which allows for higher transaction speed. This chain operates on proof of stake consensus and we will provide bonus tokens as an incentive for validators.

12. What role does the coin have?

Our native BKC token will be used as a unit of measure, medium of exchange and store of value on the platform. Any DApps built to leverage our BikeCoin platform will use our protocols and the BKC token as a medium of exchange. We will also compensate our community contributors, such as those providing computing resources using BKC tokens.

13. Who will want to buy this coin?

The native BKC token will be of interest to two groups: (a) merchants participating in the bike ecosystem on the platform, and (b) community contributors who are earning tokens for services provided to the platform.

14. What is your total addressable market?

Our addressable market includes the whole of bike sharing, which is currently a USD 5b business. This market is expanding at a phenomenal compound annual growth rate of 20%.

15. Is your platform fully decentralised?

Yes, we have decentralised the commercial activities, asset tracking and the validation of transactions. Pricing is determined by merchants using the platform. Even our computational algorithms will be published in open source so that the community can check our logic, thereby earning bug bounties.

16. How long do you estimate it will take for the network to launch?

We will release alpha and beta versions on testnets by end of 2018 and will have a public release on the mainnet by 2nd quarter 2019.

17. What exchange(s) will/do you plan to be listed on?

We will list our BKC token on decentralised exchanges and also on at least two major centralised exchanges. Decentralised exchanges do not charge listing fees and the onboarding process is much easier.

18. What is your Monetary (money supply) Policy?

BikeCoin will premine 800m tokens valued at USD 0.05 each, of which 79% will be released through the public sale (50%), block rewards (22%), community development (5%), bounty and airdrop (2%). The block rewards will be issued to validators - nodes which are hosting the transaction validation blockchain. The maximum issue size is 800m BKC tokens.

19. What is your Fiscal (tax & incentives) Policy?

To attract the broadest set of users, we will permit purchase in major cryptocurrencies and stable coins. We will offer discounts to encourage our customers to use our token and we will have a 'buy back' program, to burn a percentage of tokens received through such purchases.
